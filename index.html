About CliftonStrengths

CliftonStrengths (formerly StrengthsFinder) is an assessment tool that identifies your 34 natural talents, ranked in order. Your "Top 5" are your most dominant strengths.

These talents are grouped into four domains. Click on each domain to learn more:

Strategic Thinking

How you absorb, think about, and analyze information and situations.

Analytical +

Searches for reasons and causes; has the ability to think about all the factors that might affect a situation.

Context +

Enjoys thinking about the past; understands the present by researching its history.

Futuristic +

Inspired by the future and what could be; inspires others with their visions of the future.

Ideation +

Fascinated by ideas; able to find connections between seemingly disparate phenomena.

Input +

Has a craving to know more; often likes to collect and archive all kinds of information.

Intellection +

Characterized by their intellectual activity; introspective and appreciates intellectual discussions.

Learner +

Has a great desire to learn and wants to continuously improve. The process of learning excites them.

Strategic +

Creates alternative ways to proceed; can quickly spot the relevant patterns and issues in any scenario.

Relationship Building

How you build and nurture strong relationships with others.

Adaptability +

Prefers to "go with the flow"; tends to be a "now" person who lives in the moment.

Connectedness +

Has faith in the links between all things; believes there are few coincidences.

Developer +

Recognizes and cultivates the potential in others; spots signs of growth in others.

Empathy +

Can sense the feelings of other people by imagining themselves in others' lives or situations.

Harmony +

Looks for consensus; doesn't enjoy conflict and seeks areas of agreement.

Includer +

Is accepting of others; shows awareness of those who feel left out and tries to include them.

Individualization +

Intrigued with the unique qualities of each person; figures out how people who are different can work together.

Positivity +

Has an enthusiasm that is contagious; is upbeat and can get others excited about what they are going to do.

Relator +

Enjoys close relationships with others; finds deep satisfaction in working hard with friends to achieve a goal.

Influencing

How you take charge, speak up, and make sure your team is heard.

Activator +

Can make things happen by turning thoughts into action; is often impatient for action.

Command +

Has presence; can take control of a situation and make decisions.

Communication +

Finds it easy to put their thoughts into words; good conversationalist and presenter.

Competition +

Measures their progress against the performance of others; strives to win first place.

Maximizer +

Focuses on strengths as a way to stimulate personal and group excellence.

Self-Assurance +

Feels confident in their ability to manage their own lives; possesses an inner compass.

Significance +

Wants to be very important in the eyes of others; is independent and wants to be recognized.

Woo +

Loves the challenge of meeting new people and winning them over; "Winning Others Over".

Executing

How you make things happen and turn ideas into reality.

Achiever +

Has great stamina and works hard; gets great satisfaction from being busy and productive.

Arranger +

Can organize, but also has a flexibility; likes to figure out how all pieces can be arranged for maximum productivity.

Belief +

Has certain core values that are unchanging; from these values emerges a defined purpose for their life.

Consistency +

Keenly aware of the need to treat people the same; tries to treat everyone with equality.

Deliberative +

Describes people who are careful and vigilant; serious about decisions and choices.

Discipline +

Enjoys routine and structure; their world is best described by the order they create.

Focus +

Can take a direction, follow through, and make corrections to stay on track; prioritizes, then acts.

Responsibility +

Takes psychological ownership of what they say they will do; committed to stable values like honesty.

Restorative +

Adept at dealing with problems; good at figuring out what is wrong and resolving it.
